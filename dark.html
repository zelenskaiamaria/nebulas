dark emissions

Dark nebulae are giant clouds of dust and cold gas that lie in the space between Earth and distant stars. The clouds block some of the starlight and appear as dark patches of sky, completely or almost completely devoid of stars. The temperature of dark nebulae is -250Â°C.
Dark nebulae are formed by the forces of gravity and pressure. When a cloud of gas and dust begins to shrink due to gravity, it can split into denser regions called protostellar clouds. In these regions, dust and gas clump together to form new stars and planets.
The composition of dark nebulae includes various chemical elements and molecules. The main components are hydrogen and oxygen. In addition, elements such as carbon, nitrogen, sulfur, and other heavy elements are also present in nebulae.
